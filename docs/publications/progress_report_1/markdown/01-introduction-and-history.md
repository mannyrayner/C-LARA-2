# 1. Introduction and History (Concise Draft Plan)

## 1.1 Purpose of this report

- Provide a short first progress report on C-LARA-2, covering the first few months of work.
- Record what has already been built and what has changed in the development process.
- Avoid writing a full manual: much of the pedagogical functionality continues ideas already described for LARA and C-LARA.
- Establish a shared source document for later papers, including the increasingly likely paper on AI authorship being discussed with David Gunkel.

## 1.2 Project lineage: LARA → C-LARA → C-LARA-2

- **LARA** established the core pedagogical idea: richly annotated, multimodal language-learning texts.
- **C-LARA** added stronger AI support for text generation, annotation, and end-user content creation.
- **C-LARA-2** keeps the language-learning aim, but should be presented primarily as a new AI-centered project organization: the AI is not just helping users make content, it is also building and maintaining the platform itself.

## 1.3 What makes C-LARA-2 different

- C-LARA-2 is an AI-authored repository in the operational sense used in this project: the committed code, tests, documentation, roadmap documents, issue records, operational notes, and publication drafts are written by the AI.
- Humans provide goals, priorities, corrections, domain expertise, and acceptance decisions.
- This division of labour has made progress faster and more flexible than in C-LARA. If a design or implementation is unsatisfactory, the AI can often rewrite the relevant material within hours, sometimes within minutes.

## 1.4 Current reporting frame

- The report is an interim account, not a completion report.
- It should present C-LARA-2 as both:
  - a continuation of a language-learning content platform, and
  - an experiment in AI-authored software engineering, project management, sysadmin work, and academic writing.

## 1.5 Authorship angle for later papers

- The first report may become an important example for a later paper on AI authorship.
- The working claim to preserve carefully is that the AI appears to satisfy many formal authorship criteria for the report and related publications, except the criterion of being human.
- This should be framed as a live research and ethics question, not as a settled policy claim.
