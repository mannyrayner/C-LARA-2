# 2. Core Themes and AI Autonomy (Outline)

## 2.1 Stable core theme: multimodal pedagogical content

- The primary educational objective remains creation and delivery of multimodal texts for language learners.
- Core modalities include text, translation/glossing support, audio, and images.

## 2.2 Evolving theme: increasing AI autonomy

- AI involvement has grown across generations:
  - minimal in LARA,
  - substantial in C-LARA,
  - expanded and systematized in C-LARA-2.

## 2.3 AI role decomposition in C-LARA-2

- **Content/annotation generation**: AI-assisted writing and linguistic annotation.
- **Image generation**: style-consistent and content-consistent illustration support.
- **Code implementation**: Codex-driven implementation of repository code.
- **Project management/documentation**: AI-maintained roadmap/issues/docs workflows.
- **Publication drafting support**: AI-authored report/paper draft materials under human review.
- **Sysadmin/deployment tasks**: AI-guided operational work (including AWS setup/migration orchestration).

## 2.4 Human role (to state explicitly)

- Humans provide goals, domain expertise, critique, prioritization, and acceptance decisions.
- Humans remain accountable for correctness, safety, framing, and external claims.

## 2.5 Suggested cautionary framing

- Emphasize that autonomy claims are operational/process claims, not claims of infallibility.
- Highlight known risks: regressions, drift, hallucination, and context-loss between sessions.
